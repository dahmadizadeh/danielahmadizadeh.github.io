Hi, I’m Daniel — I’m currently growing Crustdata, a Y Combinator startup building the Google for AI agents.

In 2025, we’ve grown from $700K to $5M ARR (as of October 30). I also teach entrepreneurship at Columbia University and Stanford University. Over the last 10+ years I’ve been the CEO of two Y Combinator–backed startups, helping launch new products and change the trajectory of existing ones.

I’ve helped lead three companies to over $1M+ ARR, and my work has been featured in WSJ, The Today Show, and NPR. I’ve spoken on startups at Harvard, Princeton, and TED (where I was a TED Resident). I angel invest and coach founders. I was the first investor in Motion after their YC batch; it is now a $550M company.

Why do I teach?

My mission is to change how entrepreneurship is taught globally — shifting it from theoretical to practical. In the last three years, I’ve taught 500+ students, helping them leave the classroom more confident not just in their projects, but in their lives.

My framework, Start With One, focuses on solving a real problem for one real person as a microcosm of the entire market. It transforms entrepreneurship from idea-led to problem-led.

Start With One has guided founders to launch new ventures and measure their impact. Testimonials are here. Join my weekly office hours for live startup support.
