Hi, I'm Daniel — I'm currently growing Crustdata, a Y Combinator startup building the Google for AI agents. In 2025, we've grown from $700K to $5M ARR (as of October 30).

I also teach entrepreneurship at Columbia University and Stanford University. Over the last 10+ years I've been the CEO of two Y Combinator–backed startups. My expertise is getting new products launched and changing the trajectory of existing ones.

I have helped lead three companies to over $1M+ ARR, and my work has been recognized in publications like WSJ, The Today Show, and NPR. I've spoken at Harvard, Princeton, and TED (where I was a TED Resident). Beyond building, I also angel invest and coach founders. I was the very first investor in Motion after their YC batch at seed, now a $550M company.

My dream is to change the way entrepreneurship is taught globally — shifting it from theoretical to practical. Over three years I've taught 500+ students, helping them leave the classroom more confident in their next steps.

My framework, Start With One, focuses on solving a real problem for one real person, as a microcosm of an entire market. It transforms entrepreneurship from idea-led to problem-led, turning theoretical personas into actual human beings.

Start With One has guided founders to launch new ventures and measure their impact. See testimonials here. Join my weekly office hours for live startup advice.
